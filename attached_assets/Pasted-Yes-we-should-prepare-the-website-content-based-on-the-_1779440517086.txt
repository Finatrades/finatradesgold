# How Finatrades Works

## Step 1 — User Registration, KYC / KYB & Compliance Onboarding

Before accessing the platform, every user completes a secure onboarding process.

Finatrades supports different user types:

**Exporters / Sellers**
Companies or suppliers that want to submit commodities into the warehouse network and offer them to buyers.

**Importers / Buyers**
Companies looking to source verified commodities through the marketplace and place structured purchase orders.

**Government / Sovereign Entities**
Government bodies that may participate in strategic commodity sourcing, barter arrangements, or structured trade programs.

The onboarding process includes account creation, role selection, email or OTP verification, company profile setup, document upload, KYC/KYB review, AML and sanctions screening, risk checks, and final admin approval.

Once approved, users receive access to role-specific modules such as B2B Wallet, Trade Finance, Marketplace, Warehouse Consignment, and Government Barter.

---

## Step 2 — Seller Consignment Creation & Warehouse Submission

Sellers can create a consignment request by submitting commodity details, quantity, origin, packaging, expected arrival date, ownership documents, commercial invoices, certificates, export documents, and transport information.

The seller chooses the preferred destination warehouse and submits the goods for verification and storage readiness.

This step allows the platform to prepare the inventory before arrival, check warehouse capacity, validate documents, assign logistics coordination, and prepare the consignment for marketplace visibility after inspection.

### Key Seller Benefits

Verified ownership records
Warehouse-ready consignment process
Document validation
Better buyer visibility
Trade finance eligibility
Inventory readiness for marketplace listing

## Step 3 — Pre-Arrival Logistics, Warehouse Reception & Inventory Hub

Before the goods arrive at the destination warehouse, the logistics and warehouse teams manage shipment tracking, document verification, ETA alerts, warehouse booking, customs readiness, inspection preparation, and arrival coordination.

This stage connects shipment movement with warehouse reception.

Once goods reach the warehouse, the system records arrival details, container information, inspection status, warehouse booking, and document completion.

The objective is simple: goods should not arrive blindly. Every shipment must be tracked, documented, and prepared before warehouse reception.

## Step 7 — Government Commodities Barter Workflow

Government users may access a dedicated optional branch for strategic commodity barter.

This workflow is designed for sovereign entities that wish to exchange one commodity for another, such as crude oil, fuel products, food items, wheat, gold, fertilizer, minerals, or other strategic commodities.

The government barter flow includes government onboarding, sovereign verification, barter request creation, offered commodity definition, required commodity definition, valuation comparison, counterparty matching, negotiation, settlement difference support, and execution monitoring.

This is not the normal buyer-seller marketplace route. It is a parallel optional branch for approved government or sovereign users only.

### Suggested Government Section Heading

### **Strategic Commodity Barter for Government Users**

Finatrades supports structured government commodity barter workflows where approved sovereign users can submit strategic exchange requirements, compare valuation differences, identify counterparties, and monitor execution through a controlled digital process.

## Step 8 — Trade Finance, Escrow, Settlement & Deal Completion

Once a buyer and seller agree on commercial terms, the transaction moves into deal confirmation, trade finance review, payment confirmation, escrow lock, inventory reservation, order confirmation, warehouse release instruction, shipment milestone tracking, condition verification, seller payout, and audit trail completion.

The settlement workflow ensures that goods are not released until the required conditions are met.

Funds, inventory, documents, logistics, and delivery milestones are connected into one controlled execution flow.

### Core Settlement Rules

No verified inventory means no sale.
No funded buyer means no lock.
No escrow confirmation means no release.
No delivery milestone means no final payout.
No documents means no completed audit trail.
